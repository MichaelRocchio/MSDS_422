Management Problem:

Suppose management is thinking about using a language model to classify written customer reviews and call and complaint logs. If the most critical customer messages can be identified, then customer support personnel can be assigned to contact those customers.

How would you advise senior management? What kinds of systems and methods would be most relevant to the customer services function? Considering the results of this assignment in particular, what is needed to make an automated customer support system that is capable of identifying negative customer feelings? What can data scientists do to make language models more useful in a customer service function?

Answer:

Utalizing this to determine the most critical reviews would be an effective method to study customer service. So the first thing that I would think of with this would be how the customer data ends up in text before it can go into the language model. You would have to outsource voice to text technology and implement it in the call center which can be difficult.

You would also need, because this is a deep learning model, a huge amount of data and the necessary storage and processing power for the data science team. This being said, the length of the customer converstions would be a large factor, additionally data protection would be an issue. Alot of companies ask security questions over the phone and you dont want that stored anywhere it doesnt need to be.

Regarding how to identify negative experiences; I would really wonder how we would consider a negative customer experience and say that it all depends on the company. Who would provide that? would it be the customer, call center rep, or would you need to allocate resources to a third party( which could be internal or external) to detemine if this is the type of call that needs to be studied.

Finally, NLP models are very useful in customer service settings and it brings the capability of deep learning to that department within companies. Traditionally customer service departments were locked out of analytics because there was no good way to quantify text and feed it into an algorithm. But NLP models changed all that!

def remove_URL(text):

def bad_spelling(text):
    corrected_text = []
    misspelled_words = spell.unknown(text.split())
    for word in text.split():
        if word in misspelled_words:
            corrected_text.append(spell.correction(word))
        else:
            corrected_text.append(word)
    return " ".join(corrected_text)
